Execution Plan
12-Week Sprint Plan
Four phases from discovery to full rollout. Decision gate at Week 8 — kill or scale based on early cohort data before full rollout commitment.
⚖ Week 8 Decision Gate
At Week 8 we run a formal go/no-go review. If D30 re-engagement rate ≥15% and email unsubscribe rate <2%, we proceed to full rollout. If either metric misses, we pause, diagnose, and decide whether to iterate or kill before committing to full rollout. This gate prevents sunk-cost continuation of a failing hypothesis.

When We Walk Away
Kill Criteria
We define failure upfront — before a line of code is written. These are the conditions under which we stop, reassess, and redirect resources. Intellectual honesty over sunk-cost continuation.
Why Kill Criteria Matter
Most teams define success but not failure. Kill criteria force intellectual honesty — they prevent teams from moving goalposts when results disappoint. Defining them upfront signals product maturity and earns engineering trust. If we hit a kill condition, we don't lose — we learn fast and redirect capital to the next highest-leverage bet.
